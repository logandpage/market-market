Ad Blocking Problems

Let me start by saying that our standard setup is pretty clean: No alcohol, tobacco, games, politics. However, we do have the ability to further filter content to meet the expectations of each publisher and we will be glad to make sure to block your requests, where available. But, due to the nature of programmatic ads, not every provider has the capabilities to block by brand, but where it is possible, we will happily take care of it for you. Please be aware that blocking major companies or categories like this can have repercussions on ad revenue. If performance does decrease, keep in mind that may be why. You'll also need to understand that even when we set rules and filters accordingly - that some advertisers elect to not 'play by the rules' and will mis-categorize an ad to gain more exposure. When we encounter this, we always try our best to track down the culprit and disable them, but it can take a little while to find them throughout all the exchanges and sometimes we just aren't able to track down the offenders. We only ask that in those situations you remember to be patient with us, this industry is far from perfect and we have to work around its shortcomings.

History of RPM

The market trends in the advertising industry vary throughout the year. To help prepare you for what the rest of the year holds, here's a brief description of each of the year's quarters: Q1 (Jan-Mar): This quarter starts off with the slowest performance rates of the year. January comes right after Q4 when all of the major brands have spent their budgets on the holidays and know that customers have spent most of their flexible income. This leaves them with little incentive so they pay less or buy less advertising during the first quarter. Performance slowly increases throughout the quarter until March because people are inside most of the day and browsing the internet (obviously this may vary based on your niche audience and what they are looking for). Q2 (Apr-Jun): Quarter two follows beginning quarter lows but quickly climbs to perform around the yearly average until the summer months approach. Part of this is due to more people heading on vacation and spending more of their free time outside, not online. Q3 (Jul-Sep): Traffic and earnings remain low but can spike around holidays and improve sharply as back-to-school campaigns begin. As people return from vacation and start the regular “school session” period along with cooler weather, more individuals remain inside and begin spending more time online again. Q4 (Oct-Dec): This is almost always the best performing quarter of the year. Performance starts around yearly averages near the beginning of the quarter but steadily climbs through December. The last week of December usually sags, as ad providers have exhausted their budgets and take an industry-wide holiday.

Your Ads vs.Your User's Ads

We partner with ad providers to give them access to top quality sites, like yours. In short, we at DigiMax do not choose or provide the actual ads you or your traffic see. These impressions are generated by the ad companies that win the bids for the different ad placements on your site and in turn, the actual ad image is selected based on the individuals viewing, search and even shopping history. This is why the actual ads you see when bringing up your site are not identical to the ones your members may see. The placement and such are the same but the actual ad image may not. We hope that makes sense and helps clarify your questions about ads you may or may not be seeing.
